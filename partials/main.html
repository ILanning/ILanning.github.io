Myself

    I'm a longtime hobbyist developer with several programming courses under my belt looking to break into the industry. My first experiences with game programming came through DigiPen's high school course program. I was fortunate enough to get to attend the program on the DigiPen campus itself, which exposed me to a number of the resources that the college provides. Our teacher, Jeromy Walsh, started us off on the console with C++ covering concepts like templates, memory management, and pointers; briefly stopped over in Java; and spent the last half of the year in C# on the XNA framework. The framwork provides fairly little beyond a well-built game loop and a simplification of graphics, audio, and input calls, which made it an amazing learning tool. The second year there involved jumping to 3D and networking projects.

    Money concerns stopped me from being able to continue on to the college program itself, but I kept up programming on my own time while working for my parent's businesses. I've also done some art, giving me experience with Maya, ZBrush, Photoshop, and a number of other art programs. I've brought models through the modeling, texture-painting, and UV mapping/skinning processes, giving me experience with the technologies involved with those processes. I've also done personal research to hone my programming abilities, delving a bit in to topics such as the mechanics of talking to RAM, shaders, processor caches, and branch prediction.

    Most recently I've attended Coding Dojo, a full-time 3 and a half month program that teaches full stack web development. In this program I learned the Django library and Python; MongoDB, Express.js, Angular.js, and Node.js for Javascript; and Ruby/Ruby on Rails. These languages are dramatically different from my mainstay of C# in many ways, and going through them gave me a fresh perspective on how to tackle problems in new ways.
